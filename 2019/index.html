Modules and tentative schedule

  • Day 1: Intro to computational genomics: Operations on genomic intervals
  • Day 2: RNA-seq applications and analysis
  • Day 3: ChIP-seq applications and analysis

General Info

The course will cover data analysis for genomics. There will be theoretical lectures followed by practical sessions where students directly apply what they have learned. The programming will be mainly done in R. The course will be beneficial for first year computational biology PhD students, and experimental biologists and medical scientists who want to begin data analysis or are seeking a better understanding of computational genomics and analysis of popular sequencing methods. We have done similar courses in 2015, 2016, 2017 and 2018. Here is a link to an article about the 2015 course. Here is a link to an article about a similar course we organized in 2015.

Duration and Structure

The lectures and practicals will be held from 9am to 6pm. Lectures are followed by hands-on exercises to apply what candidates have just learnt. A computer room with preset workstations will be provided. There will be one afternoon (mid-week) reserved for a social group activity

Application

Application deadline: 02 July 2017 (extended 1 day)

Candidates must apply by filling in the online form. The form will have a section for submitting an abstract on current research interests and projects. Please be prepared to provide an abstract describing your current research projects and interest (max 1000 words).

There will also be questions on programming experience. These questions will help us organize a better course that is adjusted to applicant competencies. So, it is important you fill in those questions truthfully.

Cost

A course fee of 200 EUR will be charged to cover printed materials, lunch and snacks during the course. Payment details will be e-mailed to the candidates accepted to the course.
